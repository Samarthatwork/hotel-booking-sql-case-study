Below are the business questions solved using SQL in this case study.

---

### 1. Same-City Bookings by Customers
Find the top 5 customers who made the highest number of bookings in the same city where they live.  
Display the customer ID and the percentage of same-city bookings compared to their total bookings.

---

### 2. Female Contribution by Hotel
Calculate the percentage contribution by female customers in terms of:
- Number of bookings
- Revenue  
for each hotel.

---

### 3. Cross-State Customer Bookings
For each hotel, find the number of bookings made by customers who visited from a different state.

---

### 4. Peak Occupancy Date per Hotel
For each hotel, identify the date when the occupancy was maximum.  
A customer should not be considered present in the hotel on the checkout date.

---

### 5. Customers Visiting Multiple States
Find customers who have booked hotels in at least three different states.

---

### 6. Monthly Occupancy Rate
Calculate the occupancy rate (percentage of rooms booked out of total available rooms) for each hotel for every month.

---

### 7. Fully Occupied Dates
Identify the dates when each hotel was fully occupied.

---

### 8. Highest Revenue Booking Channel
For each hotel and each month, determine which booking channel generated the highest revenue.

---

### 9. Booking Channel Share
Find the percentage share of total bookings contributed by each booking channel.

---

### 10. Revenue by Generation
For each hotel, calculate the total revenue generated by:
- Millennials (born between 1980 and 1996)
- Gen Z customers (born after 1996)

---

### 11. Average Stay Duration
Calculate the average stay duration (in nights) for each hotel.

---

### 12. Advance Booking Behavior
Find the average number of days customers book in advance for each hotel.

---

### 13. Customers with No Bookings
Identify customers who have never made any hotel booking.

---

### 14. Multi-Hotel Stays in Same Month
Find customers who stayed in at least three distinct hotels within the same month.  
Display the customer ID, month, and number of bookings.
